Open with the point. Say what you learned or built and why it matters, in your own
voice, from your own experience. Do not warm up with a definition an AI could have
written — lead with the first-hand thing only you can say.

## Write sections for humans

Use `##` / `###` headings and normal paragraphs because that is how people read — not
because you are "chunking for AI". Well-organized prose is readability; there is no
special AI syntax to adopt. See [writing-for-ai-search.md](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/ai-optimization-guide)
for the source of truth.

- Bullets when the content is genuinely a list.
- Link to related posts internally so a reader (and a crawler) can follow the thread.
- Show a real example — a command, a diff, a number — over a vague claim.

## Close with something earned

End on the actual takeaway, not a manufactured tagline. If you edited this later, bump
`updatedAt` and say what changed.
